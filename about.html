About Thomas Woodham-Smith

Thomas Woodham-Smith is an antiques dealer and art advisor focused on quality of manufacture and originality of design across various periods. With a career spanning decades, he has established himself as a trusted voice in the international antiques market.

Career Highlights

In 1985, Thomas joined Mallett, where he quickly rose through the ranks to become associate director and later managing director of Mallett at Bourdon House. In 2006, he was appointed Managing Director at the Bond Street location, overseeing one of London's most prestigious antiques galleries.

In 2008, Thomas founded and served as Creative Director and Chairman of the Masterpiece Fair, an influential art and antiques fair that became a hallmark of the London social calendar. He continued in this role until 2010, when he left Mallett to establish himself in private dealing and consultancy.

Since transitioning to independent practice, Thomas has undertaken notable projects including collaborations with renowned photographer and designer Willy Rizzo, overseeing specialized restoration businesses, and acquiring workshop premises in Clapham to support his growing network of craftspeople and conservators.

In 2019, Thomas co-purchased and spearheaded the regeneration of the BADA Fair, rebranding and repositioning it as The Open Art Fair. This venture reflects his ongoing commitment to fostering excellence and innovation within the antiques and art community.

Philosophy

Thomas believes that the true value of an antique lies not merely in its age or provenance, but in the craftsmanship and design that created it. Whether sourcing Georgian furniture, European ceramics, or contemporary works by emerging designers, his focus remains unwavering: quality of manufacture and originality of design.
